================================================================================
  FIESTA vs Tropical Monte Carlo: Cross-Check Summary
  Date: 2026-03-18
================================================================================

This document summarizes the cross-validation between FIESTA5 (sector
decomposition with delta-function/simplex domain) and the tropical Monte
Carlo code (tropical fan decomposition on [0,∞) domain).

The two codes compute the same integrals in different representations,
related by the GL(1) gauge transformation (Cheng-Wu theorem):

  ∫₀^∞ dⁿx f(1,x₁,...,xₙ)  =  ∫_Δ dⁿt · t₀^{D-n-1} · f(t₀,t₁,...,tₙ)

where t₀ = 1-∑tᵢ, D is the homogeneity degree of the integrand, and Δ
is the simplex. This allows direct numerical comparison of both the
1/ε pole and the ε⁰ finite part.

Scripts are in: TROPICAL_MONTE_CARLO/CC/

================================================================================
  5. INVESTIGATION OF ε/ε CROSS-TERMS
================================================================================

  An initial apparent 37σ discrepancy in the finite part was traced to
  a comparison error: the MC TOTAL integral includes the G0 (pole)
  integrand as one of its sub-integrands. FIESTA's ε⁰ coefficient does
  not include the pole coefficient. The correct comparison is:

    MC finite  =  TOTAL - G0     (not just TOTAL)
    FIESTA ε⁰  =  c₀            (pole-subtracted by construction)

  The bispectrum combination formula correctly accounts for this:

    finite = pref × (TOTAL + (γ-1) × G0)
           = pref × (TOTAL - G0) + pref × γ × G0

  where:
  - (TOTAL - G0) cancels the G0 inside TOTAL
  - γ × G0 is the ε/ε cross-term from the external prefactor's
    ε-dependence (γ = PrefactorLogDeriv)

  The G1 log-insertion terms in ProcessDivergentSector correctly
  capture the integrand's internal ε-derivative:
  - Variable terms: a₁ᵢ/a₀ᵢ × log(yᵢ) for non-divergent variables
  - Polynomial terms: B₁ⱼ × log(Pⱼ) for polynomial exponents

  The flattened prefactor |det(M)|/∏a₀ᵢ is ε-independent by
  construction (flattening is performed at ε=0), so no separate
  prefactor ε-derivative is needed in G1.

  Status: No bug found. The ε/ε effect is correctly handled.


================================================================================
  OVERALL CONCLUSION
================================================================================

  All cross-checks pass. The tropical Monte Carlo code produces results
  consistent with FIESTA's independent sector decomposition for:

  - Convergent integrals (exact to ~10⁻⁶ relative error)
  - Divergent 1/ε pole coefficients (exact match in all cases)
  - ε⁰ finite parts (agreement within 2σ for the full 5D bubblepp integral)
  - Proper 1/√N MC convergence scaling

  The GL(1)/Cheng-Wu gauge transformation correctly converts between the
  [0,∞) affine chart (tropical MC) and the simplex with delta function
  (FIESTA), as verified by the paper arXiv:2204.02988 (Mizera).
