================================================================================
  TROPICAL MONTE CARLO EVALUATION PIPELINE
  Summary of tropical_eval.wl
================================================================================


1.  THE PROBLEM
================================================================================

We want to numerically evaluate generalized Euler integrals of the form

    I = Integral_{[0,inf)^n} dx_1 ... dx_n  prod_i x_i^{A_i}  prod_j P_j(x)^{B_j}

where P_j are polynomials in x_1,...,x_n with real (possibly kinematic-dependent)
coefficients, A_i are monomial exponents, and B_j are polynomial exponents
(typically negative, e.g. B_j = -2).  The exponents may depend on a regulator
eps for dimensional regularization.

The challenge is that the integrand can be singular near coordinate
hyperplanes, can have wildly varying magnitude, and must be evaluated at
many kinematic points.  Direct adaptive quadrature scales poorly in
dimension and kinematic count.


2.  THE STRATEGY: TROPICAL DECOMPOSITION + FLATTENING + MONTE CARLO
================================================================================

The pipeline has four conceptual stages:

    (a) Tropical fan  -->  (b) Sector processing  -->  (c) C++ generation  -->  (d) MC execution

Each stage is described below.


2a.  TROPICAL FAN DECOMPOSITION  (tropical_fan.wl)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Input:  The Newton polytope of the product P_1 * P_2 * ... * P_m
Output: A simplicial fan partitioning the positive orthant in R^n

The key idea from tropical geometry: the integrand's behavior as x -> 0 along
different directions is controlled by which monomial of the polynomial dominates.
The tropical (or normal) fan of the Newton polytope partitions R^n into
simplicial cones, one for each "region of dominance."

Computing the fan:  The fan is determined by the Newton polytope of the product
of all polynomials in the integrand.  Crucially:

  - The fan depends only on which monomials are present (the support), not on
    their coefficients or on the exponents B_j.
  - The normal fan of a Minkowski sum a*Newt(P1) + b*Newt(P2) is the common
    refinement of the individual normal fans, and this refinement is the same
    for ANY positive a, b.
  - Therefore, to compute the fan, simply pass the product P_1 * P_2 * ... * P_m
    (each to the first power) to PolytopeVertices.  No powers are needed.
  - For integrands with kinematic-dependent coefficients (e.g. P = 1 + lam*x1^2),
    use a unit-coefficient proxy (e.g. 1 + x1^2) since the fan is independent
    of coefficient values.

In code:
    fanPoly = P1 * P2 * ... * Pm;
    verts   = PolytopeVertices[fanPoly^(-1), vars];
    fanData = ComputeDecomposition[verts];

The package tropical_fan.wl computes this via Polymake.  It returns:
  - dualVertices:  a list of ray vectors (rows), each a direction in R^n
  - simplexList:   a list of simplices, each a list of indices into dualVertices

Each simplex defines one sector (simplicial cone) of the decomposition.  Since
the fan covers all of R^n, the sum over sectors reconstructs the original integral.


2b.  SECTOR PROCESSING  (Module 1: ProcessSector)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

For each sector (simplicial cone with rays rho_1,...,rho_n), we perform:

Step 1: Monomial change of variables.

    x_i = prod_j  y_j^{M_{ij}}     where  M = -Transpose[{rho_1,...,rho_n}]

    This maps [0,inf)^n -> [0,1)^n (after further flattening) and aligns the
    cone with coordinate axes.  The Jacobian contributes:

        dx_1...dx_n = |det(M)| * prod_j y_j^{sum_i M_{ij} - 1} dy_1...dy_n

    Combined with the original monomial prefactor x_i^{A_i}, the new monomial
    prefactor is:

        prod_j y_j^{rawA_j - 1}    where  rawA = (A + 1) . M

Step 2: Tropical factoring.

    After substitution, each polynomial P_k becomes a sum of monomials in y with
    SIGNED exponents.  The tropically dominant monomial (the one the fan says
    dominates in this cone) has the minimum exponents:

        d_{k,i} = min_m (exponent of y_i in monomial m of P_k)

    We factor it out:

        P_k(y) = prod_i y_i^{d_{k,i}}  *  Q_k(y)

    The "cleared" polynomial Q_k has all non-negative y-exponents and includes
    a constant term (the dominant monomial itself).  The factored-out y-powers
    merge into the monomial prefactor, giving effective exponents:

        a_i^eff = rawA_i + sum_k B_k * d_{k,i}

    IMPORTANT: The tropical factoring uses only exponent vectors, not
    coefficients.  This is correct because the fan structure is determined by
    tropicalization (which ignores coefficients).  With large coefficients, the
    tropically dominant monomial may not be the numerically largest one, but
    the factoring is an exact algebraic identity -- correctness is unaffected,
    only MC variance changes.

    For a properly constructed tropical fan and convergent integral,
    Re(a_i^eff) > 0 for all i, guaranteeing integrability.

Step 3: Flattening substitution.

    y_i = (y'_i)^{1/a_i^eff}

    This absorbs the monomial prefactor entirely:

        y_i^{a_i^eff - 1} dy_i  =  (1/a_i^eff) dy'_i

    After flattening:
    - Integration domain is [0,1]^n
    - NO monomial prefactor remains (it's exactly 1)
    - The prefactor becomes |det(M)| / prod(a_i^eff)
    - Each monomial {coeff, {e_1,...,e_n}} of Q_k becomes {coeff, {e_1/a_1,...,e_n/a_n}}
    - The integrand is bounded on [0,1]^n and O(1) -- ideal for uniform MC

The sector integral becomes:

    I_sector = prefactor * Integral_{[0,1]^n} dy'  prod_j Q_j(y')^{B_j}

2d.  C++ CODE GENERATION  (Module 3: GenerateCppMonteCarlo)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Mathematica-side symbolic processing produces, for each sector, a list of
{coefficient, exponentVector} pairs for the flattened polynomial.  Module 3
converts this into a self-contained C++ source file.

Expression conversion (MmaToC):
    A recursive pattern-matching converter translates Mathematica expressions
    to C++ strings:
        Integer n  -->  "n.0"
        Rational p/q  -->  "(p.0/q.0)"
        Complex[a,b]  -->  "cx(a, b)"        (cx = std::complex<double>)
        Power[x, n]  -->  "std::pow(x, n)"
        Log[x]  -->  "std::log(x)"
        Symbol lam  -->  "params[i]"          (kinematic parameter lookup)

    Kinematic symbols are mapped to a params[] array so the same compiled
    binary evaluates at any kinematic point.

Polynomial evaluation (log-exp pattern):
    Each flattened polynomial Q_j(y') is evaluated as:

        double log_y[dim];
        for (i = 0; i < dim; i++) log_y[i] = std::log(y[i]);

        cx P0(0.0, 0.0);
        P0 += coeff_0 * std::exp(alpha_0_0 * log_y[0] + alpha_0_1 * log_y[1]);
        P0 += coeff_1 * std::exp(alpha_1_0 * log_y[0]);
        ...

    This avoids pow(y, fractional) and handles y near 0 gracefully.

One type of integrand function is generated (v2, convergent only):

    integrand_conv_N   -- convergent sector:  prefactor * prod Q_j^{B_j}

All functions have the same signature: cx f(const double* y, const double* params).
They are collected in a function pointer table.

The generated main() function:
    1. Reads kinematic points from an input file (N_PARAMS doubles per line)
    2. Loops over kinematic points (OpenMP parallelized when available)
    3. For each kinematic point, loops over all integrand functions
    4. For each function, draws n_samples uniform points on [0,1]^dim
    5. Accumulates mean and variance using Welford's online algorithm
    6. Sums sector contributions (means add, variances add by independence)
    7. Writes one line per kinematic point: re  im  err_re  err_im
    8. Optional debug mode (-DTROPICAL_MC_DEBUG) adds NaN detection and
       magnitude monitoring

Compilation:
    CompileCpp invokes g++ with C++17.  If OpenMP is unavailable (e.g. macOS
    clang), it automatically retries without -fopenmp.  The generated C++
    wraps all OpenMP calls in #ifdef _OPENMP guards.  An optional 4th argument
    (cubaPrefix) adds -I/-L/-lcuba for the VEGAS path; omitted/None leaves the
    MC compile line byte-identical to before.


2e.  NUMERICAL INTEGRATOR: MC vs VEGAS  ("Integrator" option)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The decomposition produces the VALUE; the sampler is a second-order optimization
on top.  GenerateCppMonteCarlo / EvaluateTropicalMC / EvaluateTropicalMCLifted
take "Integrator" -> "MC" (default) | "VEGAS":

    "MC"     plain uniform Monte Carlo (the historical default; unchanged).
    "VEGAS"  CUBA-Vegas adaptive importance sampling.  Returns the SAME value,
             typically 1-3 orders more accurate at a smaller eval budget.

The TEST/ sampler study (REPORT.md / RESULTS.md) found Vegas best on the
post-flattening integrand for n=2..8: it absorbs the sqrt(.) cube-edge structure
that flattening leaves behind, keeps a QMC-like rate (~N^-1..-1.7 vs MC's
N^-0.5) that is stable in dimension, and is the only method that scales to
n=7,8.  Implementation notes:
    - ncomp=2 (Re/Im): complex integrands are first-class.
    - n_samples is reused as Vegas maxeval per sector; output contract
      (re im reErr imErr, summed in quadrature over sectors) is unchanged.
    - VEGAS tuning options: "VegasEpsRel" (Automatic => 10^-PrecisionGoal,
      clamped [1e-12,1e-2]), "VegasSeed" (0 => Sobol), "VegasNStart",
      "VegasNIncrease", "VegasNBatch", "VegasMinEval".
    - Requires CUBA (cuba.h + libcuba; brew install cuba).  Absent => the driver
      fires TropicalEval::nocuba and returns $Failed (no silent MC fallback).
    - With "MC" the generated source, flags, and results are byte-identical to
      the pre-VEGAS pipeline (regression-gated).
LIFTED sectors (auxiliary-variable method, §8): VEGAS works WITH the lift (the
domain indicator is integrator-agnostic).  The lift's hard domain cutoff is a
discontinuity, so Vegas keeps a small residual bias that SHRINKS with budget,
and BOTH samplers' error bars are optimistic in this heavy-tail regime.  Which
wins depends on the coefficient (lifted (1+c x1^2+x2^2+x1 x2^2)^-2, k=2, 2e6
evals each; test_vegas.wl cases L1-L3):
    c = 1e-4 (small):  MC ~1.2%   VEGAS ~0.35%   -> VEGAS
    c = 1e-6 (small):  MC ~13% (error bar lies)  VEGAS ~3% -> VEGAS (decisively)
    c = 1e6  (large):  MC ~0.3%   VEGAS ~1%      -> MC
For SMALL coefficients (the main reason to lift) lifting + VEGAS BEATS lifting +
MC; for a LARGE coefficient MC edges it.  Cross-check against a reference rather
than trusting the quoted error bar.  On the standard CONTINUOUS sectors VEGAS is
unconditionally 1-3 orders tighter.  Demonstrated case-by-case in
EXAMPLES/test_vegas.wl (V1-V4 continuous, L1-L3 lifted; requires CUBA).


3.  DRIVER AND VALIDATION  (Modules 4 and 5)
================================================================================


3a.  EvaluateTropicalMC  (Module 4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The top-level driver that orchestrates the full pipeline:

    Step 1:   Validate inputs (dimensions, finiteness); error if RegulatorSymbol present
    Step 2:   ProcessSector for ALL simplicial cones
    Step 3:   If RunChecks: ValidateDecomposition on first few kinematic points
    Step 4:   Error if any divergent sectors detected (convergent-only in v2)
    Step 5:   [removed in v2]
    Step 6:   Generate C++ code (convergent sectors only)
    Step 7:   If RunChecks: debug compile and run on subset
    Step 8:   Write kinematic data file
    Step 9:   Release compile
    Step 10:  Run binary, read results
    Step 11:  Assemble final results
    Step 12:  Error summary and optional NIntegrate cross-check


3b.  Validation  (Module 5: RunAllTests)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6 tests verify end-to-end correctness (convergent integrals only in v2):

    Test 1:  Convergent 2D, real exponents (A = 2, 3)
        P = 1 + 2x1^2 + x2^2 + x1*x2^2 + 3*x1^2*x2
        Compares sector sum vs. direct NIntegrate.
    Test 2:  Convergent 2D, complex exponents (A = 2 + 0.5i)
        Same polynomial, complex power.  Tests log-exp evaluation with
        complex-valued integrand.
    Test 3v2: Negative tests (regulator guard and divergent-input guard)
        Part A: eps-regulated spec -> must return $Failed with noregulator message.
        Part B: convergent-spec but divergent sectors -> must return $Failed
                with divergentinput message.
    Test 5:  End-to-end kinematic scan (100 lambda values)
        P = 1 + lam*x1^2 + x2^2 + x1*x2^2, complex exponent
        Verifies sector decomposition at 5 representative points.
    Test 6:  Large polynomial coefficients
        Three cases with coefficients spanning up to 8 orders of magnitude
        (10^{-4} to 10^8).  Verifies that the tropical algorithm is correct
        despite the tropically dominant monomial not being the numerically
        largest one.
    Test 7:  Higher-dimensional integrals (3D and 4D)
        Case A (3D): P = 1 + x1^2 + x2^2 + x3^2 + x1*x2*x3, exponent -3
            6 rays, 8 sectors.
        Case B (4D): P = 1+x1^2+x2^2+x3^2+x4^2+x1*x2+x3*x4, exponent -4
            8 rays, 14 sectors.
        Verifies ProcessSector, tropical factoring, and
        ValidateDecomposition in dimensions > 2.

Tests 8-22 (divergent-integral and IBP tests) have been removed in v2.
Use the original TROPICAL_MONTE_CARLO package for those.

CUBA cross-check of the suite (EXAMPLES/test_cuba.wl):
    Every convergent integral of the suite — Test 1 (A=2,3), Test 2,
    Test 5 (5 representative lam values), Test 6 (Cases A/B/C, plus the
    lifted MC re-check of Case A = Test 23B), Test 7 (3D and 4D), and the
    lifted-test integrals 23A and 23D — is integrated independently with
    CUBA Cuhre applied DIRECTLY to the original integrand (x = t/(1-t)
    compactification, no tropical decomposition) and compared against the
    tropical sector sum, the tropical C++ MC, and exact values where
    available.  Gates: sector sum within 1% of Cuhre (5% for the PG2
    cases 6C and 2% for 4D), MC within 5 sigma; Test 6B's plain MC is
    reported without a gate (small constant term 10^-4 -> heavy tail,
    cf. Example 20).  Test 3v2 contains only negative tests (nothing to
    integrate).  Requires CUBA (`brew install cuba`); runtime ~5-15 min.
    Measured at the time of writing: ALL 16 CASES PASS.  Sector sums agree
    with Cuhre to 4e-6..4e-4 (1.3e-3 for the PG2 case 6C); gated MCs land
    at 0.05-1.9 sigma.  Notable Cuhre references: Test 6A = 7.850075e-4,
    Test 6B = 144.0578 +/- 1.3e-4, Test 6C = 1.684581e-3; Cuhre also
    resolves Test 23A's boundary sliver (500000 to 2e-8) and Test 23D's
    1e-6-wide peak (1e-6 to 1e-10).  Test 6B's plain MC shows the
    documented heavy tail: 175.7 +/- 60.0 (0.53 sigma, uninformative).


4.  FUNCTION REFERENCE
================================================================================


PUBLIC FUNCTIONS (TropicalEval` context)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ProcessSector[integrandSpec, dualVertices, simplex, coneIndex]
    Performs monomial change of variables, tropical factoring, and flattening
    for one simplicial cone.
    Returns: Association with keys ConeIndex, RayMatrix, DetM, NewExponents,
    MinExponents, ClearedPolys, FlattenedPolys, Prefactor, IsDivergent,
    DivergentVariable, Dimension, PolynomialExponents, etc.

CheckFlatteningMagnitude[sectorData, nSamples]
    Evaluates the flattened integrand at random points on [0,1]^n and
    reports min/max/mean magnitude.  Useful for detecting high-variance
    sectors (e.g. from large polynomial coefficients).

ValidateDecomposition[integrandSpec, fanData, testKinematics, precisionGoal]
    Computes the original integral via direct NIntegrate on [0,inf)^n, then
    computes each sector integral via NIntegrate on [0,1]^n, and compares.
    Returns: Association with DirectResult, SectorSum, RelativeError,
    SectorResults.

MmaToC[expr, paramMap]
    Converts a Mathematica expression to a C++ code string using recursive
    pattern matching.  The paramMap association maps kinematic symbols to
    "params[i]" strings.

GenerateCppMonteCarlo[convergentSectors, divergentSectors, integrandSpec, outputFile]
    Generates a complete, self-contained C++ source file with all integrand
    functions, function pointer table, file I/O, and main() with OpenMP
    parallelization and Welford variance estimation.
    Options: "NSamples" (default 10^6), "MaxDim" (default 20), "SeedBase" (42).
    The generated binary also accepts an OPTIONAL 5th argv (seed_base) that
    overrides the compile-time SeedBase at run time:
        tropical_mc <in> <out> [n_samples] [n_threads] [seed_base]
    The per-kinematic-point RNG seed is seed_base + kp.  This lets a caller
    re-run the SAME compiled binary under different seeds without recompiling.

CompileCpp[srcFile, outputBinary, debug]
    Compiles the generated C++ code.  Automatically retries without -fopenmp
    if the compiler doesn't support it (e.g. macOS clang).
    debug=True adds -DTROPICAL_MC_DEBUG for NaN detection and diagnostics.

EvaluateTropicalMC[integrandSpec, fanData, kinematicPoints, opts]
    Top-level driver.  Takes an integrand specification and fan data,
    processes all sectors, generates C++, compiles, runs, reads results,
    and returns a list of result associations.
    Options: NSamples, NThreads, RunChecks, PrecisionGoal, WorkingDirectory,
             Verbose, SeedBase (default 42).
    SeedBase is forwarded to GenerateCppMonteCarlo and ALSO passed as the runtime
    5th argv to the production binary, so distinct SeedBase values give distinct
    MC streams (verified via the z0-sweep harness; SANDBOX/z0_sweep_common.wl
    re-runs the compiled binary across >=5 seeds without recompiling).
    Note: RegulatorSymbol must be None (or absent); errors on divergent sectors.

ParsePolynomial[poly, vars]
    Parses a symbolic polynomial into a list of {coefficient, exponentVector}
    pairs.

RunAllTests[]
    Runs the 6-test validation suite (Tests 1, 2, 3v2, 5, 6, 7) with structured
    PASS/FAIL reporting.  All divergent-integral tests removed in v2.  Defined
    in tropical_eval_examples.wl (which does NOT call it); invoke it via
    `wolframscript -file EXAMPLES/run_validation_suite.wl`.

[IBP functions removed in v2: IBPReduceSector, IBPProcessSector,
IBPCheckBoundary, ValidateIBP, GenerateCppMonteCarloIBP, EvaluateTropicalMCIBP.
Use TROPICAL_MONTE_CARLO for divergent/eps-regulated integrals.]


DATA STRUCTURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IntegrandSpec (Association):
    "Polynomials"        -> {P1, P2, ...}       symbolic polynomials in x[i]
    "MonomialExponents"  -> {A1, A2, ...}       one per variable
    "PolynomialExponents"-> {B1, B2, ...}       one per polynomial
    "Variables"          -> {x[1], x[2], ...}
    "KinematicSymbols"   -> {lam, mu, ...}      or {} if none
    "RegulatorSymbol"    -> eps                  or None

SectorData (Association, returned by ProcessSector):
    "ConeIndex"          -> integer
    "RayMatrix"          -> M (n x n matrix)
    "DetM"               -> det(M)
    "SelectedRays"       -> {rho_1, ..., rho_n}
    "RawExponents"       -> rawA (before tropical factoring)
    "NewExponents"       -> effectiveA (after tropical factoring)
    "MinExponents"       -> {{d_{1,1},...,d_{1,n}}, ...} per polynomial
    "TransformedPolys"   -> monomials in y-space (signed exponents)
    "ClearedPolys"       -> monomials after factoring (non-negative exponents)
    "FlattenedPolys"     -> monomials after flattening (exps divided by a_eff)
    "Prefactor"          -> |det(M)| / prod(a_i^eff)
    "IsDivergent"        -> True/False
    "DivergentVariable"  -> index k (0 if convergent)
    "Dimension"          -> n
    "PolynomialExponents"-> {B1, ...}

[DivergentSectorData and IBPSectorData structures removed in v2.]

8.  AUXILIARY-VARIABLE LIFTING  (Phase 3 addition)
================================================================================

8a.  MOTIVATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When a polynomial has one coefficient that is orders of magnitude larger than
the others (e.g. P = 1 + 10^6 x1^2 + x2^2), the tropical fan is built from
the unit-coefficient proxy (the fan is coefficient-independent).  However, the
numerically dominant monomial in a given region may differ from the tropically
dominant monomial.  This mismatch does not affect correctness (the tropical
factoring is an exact identity) but can increase the per-sample variance.

The "auxiliary-variable lifting" technique replaces a selected extreme
coefficient C in monomial x^alpha by an auxiliary variable z^k with anchor
z0 = Abs[C]^(1/k) (real); for real positive C this is just z0 = C^(1/k) and
C = z0^k, and for complex C the phase is absorbed into a residual coefficient
(§8j).  The integral is then:

    I = Integral dz delta(z - z0) * Integral[lifted integrand]

The delta function is resolved analytically via the delta-elimination
procedure (§8c below), and the resulting lifted integrand is evaluated
on the (n+1)-dimensional tropical fan of the lifted polynomial.  In many
cases this produces a substantial reduction in per-sample variance.


8b.  NEW PUBLIC FUNCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DetectExtremeCoefficients[integrandSpec, threshold]
    Scans all polynomial monomials (via ParsePolynomial) and returns every
    monomial whose NUMERIC coefficient satisfies Abs[C] < 1/threshold or
    Abs[C] > threshold (default threshold 1000).  Symbolic / kinematic
    coefficients are skipped (magnitude unknown at lift time).
    Returns a list of Associations, each with keys:
        "PolyIndex"       -> which polynomial (1-indexed)
        "ExponentVector"  -> exponent vector of the extreme monomial
        "Coefficient"     -> the coefficient value (may be complex)
        "Magnitude"       -> Abs[N[coefficient]]  (complex modulus)
        "SuggestedK"      -> auto-chosen lift power via the anchor rule
    SuggestedK automates the anchor z0 = Abs[C]^(1/k): the "AnchorRule" option
    (default "kStar") returns k* = max(1, Ceiling[|log Abs[C]| / log threshold]),
    the smallest k that pulls z0 back inside the non-extreme band [1/thr, thr];
    "Unit" restores the legacy SuggestedK -> 1, and a function f[mag, threshold]
    supplies a custom rule.  The optional "BandEdgeGuard" (default False) adds +1
    within one decade of the band edge.  Because Magnitude uses Abs[C], the rule
    works unchanged for COMPLEX coefficients (k scales with |log Abs[C]|).
    Useful for identifying which coefficients to lift.

LiftCoefficients[integrandSpec, liftRules]
    Applies the lifting transformation to a list of rules.  Each rule is an
    Association with keys "PolyIndex", "ExponentVector", "k".
    Returns an Association with keys:
        "LiftedSpec"      -> new IntegrandSpec with the auxiliary variable z
                            appended to "Variables" (symbol x[n+1])
        "LiftData"        -> Association with keys:
                               "z0"           -> anchor z0 = Abs[C]^(1/k), real
                                                 and exact (uses the modulus, so
                                                 valid for complex C)
                               "AuxIndex"     -> index of the new variable (n+1)
                               "AuxVariable"  -> x[n+1]
                               "Rules"        -> the list of lift rules applied
                               "Residuals"    -> residual coefficients
                                                 c_i = C_i / z0^{k_i}.  For a
                                                 complex C the residual carries
                                                 the PHASE (c = e^{i arg C} for
                                                 the primary rule, |c| = 1);
                                                 keeps the lift exact when
                                                 several monomials share z0.
                               "OriginalSpec" -> copy of the input spec
    A round-trip identity check (lifted polynomial at z -> z0 equals the
    original) fires TropicalEval::liftidentity on failure; it holds exactly for
    complex C because c_i z0^{k_i} = C_i.

ProcessSectorLifted[liftedSpec, dualVertices, simplex, coneIndex, liftData]
    Extends ProcessSector to the (n+1)-dimensional lifted problem.  After
    computing the standard sector data, it performs the delta-elimination step:
    it identifies a pivot variable p (the variable to integrate against the
    delta function) and performs the monomial substitution y_p = z0 * h(y_{-p})
    to reduce the dimension by 1.

    Pivot selection (AMENDED ranking -- owner-approved, deviates from §3.5):
        Priority 1: prefer pivots that preserve the constant term after
                    re-clearing (HasConstantTerm = True).
        Priority 2: among those, prefer |m_p| = 1 (unit pivot exponent).
        Priority 3: among ties, prefer max min Re[atilde_i] (best integrability
                    margin).
    The pivot ranking was amended from the original plan (which put |m_p|=1
    first) to reduce the risk of infinite true variance from heavy-tailed
    sectors.

    Returns: SectorData Association (same schema as ProcessSector) extended
    with lifted-specific keys:
        "HasConstantTerm" -> True/False
        "EmptyDomain"     -> True if the delta constraint is inconsistent
                            (no valid y_p satisfying the substitution)
        "PivotIndex"      -> p
        "PivotExponent"   -> m_p
        "Atilde"          -> the (n-1)-vector of effective exponents after
                            the monomial substitution

    If no admissible pivot exists: fires TropicalEval::liftnopivot, returns
    $Failed.
    If pivot leads to complex-valued substitution: fires TropicalEval::liftcomplex,
    returns $Failed.
    If the resulting atilde has non-positive real part: fires
    TropicalEval::liftdivergent, returns $Failed.

ValidateLiftedDecomposition[liftedSpec, fanData, liftData, testKinematics, precisionGoal]
    Validates a lifted sector decomposition.  Runs each lifted sector through
    ProcessSectorLifted, sums the sector NIntegrate results, compares against
    direct NIntegrate of the original (unlifted) integral.
    Returns: Association with keys DirectResult, SectorSum, RelativeError,
    SectorResults, DroppedSectors (count of EmptyDomain drops).

FlattenSector[sectorData]
    (Internal helper, also available publicly.)  Given a SectorData association
    that has already had ProcessSectorLifted applied, applies the flattening
    substitution to the 2D cleared polynomial.  Used by ValidateLiftedDecomposition
    and EvaluateTropicalMCLifted.

EvaluateTropicalMCLifted[integrandSpec, kinematicPoints, opts]
    Top-level driver for the lifted pipeline.  Analogous to EvaluateTropicalMC
    but applies the auxiliary-variable lifting before sector processing.
    Options (in addition to all EvaluateTropicalMC options):
        "LiftRules"  -> list of lift-rule Associations (required if lifting;
                       each has "PolyIndex", "ExponentVector", "k").
                       Default: Automatic (calls DetectExtremeCoefficients
                       and selects the most extreme coefficient).
        "FanData"    -> explicit fan for the LIFTED polynomial.  Default:
                       Automatic (calls PolytopeVertices + ComputeDecomposition
                       on the lifted polynomial).  Must be provided explicitly
                       when the lifted Newton polytope is lower-dimensional
                       (degenerate polytope; see §8f).
    Returns: Association with key "Results" containing a list of per-kinematic-
    point Associations.  Each result association has keys:
        "Re"    -> real part of the MC estimate
        "ReErr" -> standard error on the real part
        "Im"    -> imaginary part
        "ImErr" -> standard error on the imaginary part
    Additional top-level keys: "ConvergentSectors".


8c.  DELTA-ELIMINATION PROCEDURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Given the lifted polynomial P_lift(y1,...,y_n, z) and the anchor z0, the
delta(z - z0) factor is resolved as follows.

1.  Compute the (n+1)-dimensional sector data via ProcessSector.  This gives
    a cleared polynomial in (y1,...,y_{n+1}) and effective exponents
    a_eff = (a1,...,a_{n+1}).

2.  The delta function in the original z-variable translates (via the monomial
    substitution z = prod_j y_j^{M_{n+1,j}}) to a delta constraint relating
    the y_j variables.

3.  Select a pivot p in {1,...,n+1}.  The pivot variable y_p is expressed in
    terms of the other y_j via the delta constraint:

        y_p = z0^{1/m_p} * prod_{j!=p} y_j^{-M_{n+1,j}/m_p}

    where m_p = M_{n+1,p} is the z-row entry for the pivot variable.

4.  Substitute into the cleared polynomial.  This may require re-clearing
    (if the substitution introduces negative y-exponents) and yields a
    2-variable polynomial Q_tilde(y_{i1}, y_{i2}) with effective exponents
    atilde.

5.  If Re(atilde_i) > 0 for all i (and the domain constraint is non-empty),
    the sector is convergent and contributed normally to the MC sum.

6.  If the domain constraint z0^{1/m_p} * (...) is inconsistent (e.g. requires
    y_p outside [0,inf)), the sector is tagged EmptyDomain and dropped.


8d.  NEW ERROR MESSAGES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TropicalEval::liftidentity
    Fired by LiftCoefficients when the round-trip identity check fails: the
    lifted polynomial evaluated at z -> z0 does not reproduce the original.
    (Holds exactly for real OR complex C, since c_i z0^{k_i} = C_i.)

TropicalEval::liftnopivot
    Fired by ProcessSectorLifted when no admissible pivot can be found:
    all candidate pivots produce atilde with a non-positive component.
    The sector returns $Failed and is skipped.

TropicalEval::liftcomplex
    Fired by ProcessSectorLifted when all candidate pivots produce a complex
    effective exponent atilde, so no real-valued domain indicator can be
    emitted.  This happens for complex polynomial EXPONENTS B (atilde inherits
    Im[B]), NOT for complex coefficients: a complex COEFFICIENT leaves atilde
    real (it depends only on integer exponents and real A,B) and is fully
    supported.  The sector returns $Failed and is skipped.  (See §8j.)

TropicalEval::liftdivergent
    Fired when all admissible pivots give Re(atilde_i) <= 0 for some i,
    indicating the lifted sector integral diverges.  The sector returns
    $Failed and is skipped.

TropicalEval::liftfandim
    Fired when the supplied FanData has simplex dimension inconsistent
    with the (n+1)-dimensional lifted problem (e.g. simplices of length
    n+1 expected but length n provided).

TropicalEval::liftdegenerate
    Fired by EvaluateTropicalMCLifted when Automatic fan detection is
    requested but the lifted polynomial has fewer than (n+2) monomials or
    its Newton polytope is lower-dimensional.  In this case Polymake cannot
    produce a full-dimensional simplicial fan.  The caller must supply an
    explicit fan via the "FanData" option (see §8f).


8e.  USING THE LIFTDATA DRIVER OPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EvaluateTropicalMCLifted accepts a "LiftRules" option which specifies which
monomial(s) to lift and with what k-value.  Internally the driver calls
LiftCoefficients to build the LiftData structure.  Users can inspect the
LiftData (returned by LiftCoefficients) to understand:

  - Which auxiliary variable was introduced (AuxVariable)
  - What anchor value z0 = Abs[C]^(1/k) was used (and, for complex C, the
    phase residuals c_i = C_i/z0^{k_i})
  - The lifted integrand specification (LiftedSpec)
  - The original specification (OriginalSpec)

The "FanData" option accepts either Automatic (the default) or an explicit
{dualVertices, simplexList} pair.  Explicit fan data is required whenever
the lifted Newton polytope is lower-dimensional (§8f).


8f.  DEGENERATE-LIFTED-POLYTOPE LIMITATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When the polynomial to be lifted has very few distinct monomials, the lifted
polynomial P_lift = P[z^k replacing C*x^alpha] may also have few monomials.
If the lifted polynomial has fewer monomials than (n+2) in (n+1) variables,
or if those monomials lie in a lower-dimensional affine subspace of R^{n+1},
the Newton polytope is lower-dimensional and Polymake cannot produce a
full-dimensional simplicial fan.

In this case EvaluateTropicalMCLifted fires TropicalEval::liftdegenerate and
returns $Failed unless the caller supplies an explicit fan via "FanData".

Example: P = 1 + 10^8 x1^3 x2 + x2^3 (Test 6 Case C).  After lifting the
10^8 x1^3*x2 monomial with z^k, the lifted polynomial has exactly 3 monomials
in (x1,x2,z)-space.  Their exponent vectors {0,0,0}, {0,3,0}, {3,1,k} span
only a 2D affine subspace of R^3.  For any k, the automatic fan detection
fires liftdegenerate.

The explicit fan must cover all of R^3.  For this polynomial (lineality direction
L = (k,0,-3) for z_0 = 10^(8/k)), a valid 5-ray + 6-simplex fan is:
    Rays: {1,0,0}, {0,1,0}, {-1,-3,0}, {k,0,-3}, {-k,0,3}
    Simplices: {1,2,4},{2,3,4},{3,1,4},{1,2,5},{2,3,5},{3,1,5}
(Validated via ValidateLiftedDecomposition, RelErr < 1%.)

IMPORTANT CAVEAT: For this polynomial, ALL lifted sectors have
HasConstantTerm=False regardless of k.  This is because after delta-elimination
via the z-coordinate pivot, the re-cleared polynomial in (x1,x2) lacks a
constant term.  This means:
  - The integrand is not in L^2 on [0,1]^2 for B << 0, so the true variance
    may be infinite.
  - The sample-based sigma (from finite MC) underestimates the true sigma.
  - The MC estimate may significantly underestimate the true integral value.
  - Variance comparison between unlifted and lifted is unreliable for this case.

This is a structural limitation of the current implementation when lifting a
polynomial whose support has an extreme geometry.


8g.  PIVOT RANKING DEVIATION FROM PLAN §3.5
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The original migration plan (§3.5) specified the following pivot ranking:
    (1) |m_p| = 1  (unit pivot exponent)
    (2) max min Re[atilde_i]  (best integrability margin)

After testing on Toy 1 and Toy 2 (see SANDBOX/phase1_results.txt), the ranking
was amended (owner-approved) to:
    (1) HasConstantTerm = True after re-clearing  (constant-term preservation)
    (2) |m_p| = 1
    (3) max min Re[atilde_i]

The amendment is motivated by the observation that sectors with
HasConstantTerm=False can have infinite true variance (L^2 non-integrability
near the boundary), which makes them far more damaging to MC convergence than
sectors with slightly worse atilde margin.  The amended ranking is implemented
in ProcessSectorLifted and reflected in the test suite (Test 23, §8h).

8j.  COMPLEX COEFFICIENTS  (Examples 24-27, Test 24)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The coefficient C being lifted may itself be COMPLEX (the original package and
the lifting benchmarks above used only real C).  This is fully supported, and
exact, because lifting splits C cleanly into a magnitude and a phase:

    z0 = Abs[C]^(1/k)         (REAL, by construction -- uses the modulus)
    residual c = C / z0^k     (carries the PHASE: c = e^{i arg C}, |c| = 1 for
                               the primary rule; O(1) for the secondaries)

so the lifted monomial is c * z^k * x^alpha with an O(1) complex coefficient c,
and c * z0^k = C makes the round-trip identity (checked by LiftCoefficients)
exact.  The KEY POINT is that everything which must stay real to define the
sector geometry is untouched:

  - the Newton polytope, fan, ray matrices and |det M| are integer data;
  - the effective exponents a_eff = rawA + sum_k B_k d_k, the post-delta atilde,
    the convergence gate Re(a_eff) > 0, and the domain indicator coefficients
    all depend only on those integers and the REAL exponents A_i, B_k -- never
    on the coefficient values;
  - only the polynomial VALUES Q_k(y) become complex, and the whole C++ pipeline
    already carries those as std::complex<double> (log-exp evaluation, complex
    prefactor/result).  GenerateCppMonteCarlo emits a complex numeric coefficient
    robustly as cx(re, im) (helper EmitCoeff); the real-coefficient codegen is
    byte-for-byte unchanged.

The lift therefore moves the (small) MAGNITUDE into the deterministic geometry /
prefactor exactly as in the real case, while the O(1) phase rides along in the
sampled integrand without inflating variance.

Contrast -- complex EXPONENTS are NOT liftable.  A complex polynomial exponent
B_k makes atilde complex, which admits no real-valued domain indicator, so
ProcessSectorLifted fires TropicalEval::liftcomplex (§8d).  Complex
COEFFICIENTS: supported.  Complex EXPONENTS: not (use the unlifted pipeline,
cf. Example 23).

Branch-cut proviso.  Complex Q_k are integrated on the principal branch of
log Q_k, which is the correct, NIntegrate-consistent value as long as no Q_k
winds around 0 on [0,1]^n -- automatic when the dominant constant term keeps
Q_k near the positive real axis (e.g. a small complex perturbation of a positive
polynomial), exactly the regime where lifting is wanted.
